Research Problem

Tackling Global Food Waste Challenge

Food waste has emerged as a major global issue, with approximately one-third of all food produced worldwide being discarded. Much of this waste occurs in households due to poor storage practices, misunderstanding of expiration dates, and lack of real-time monitoring.

The environmental impact includes rising greenhouse gas emissions and inefficient resource use. Despite smart home technologies, there's a lack of integrated, user-friendly solutions for real-time food freshness monitoring.

  • Hybrid image + gas sensor detection system
  • ML-powered shelf-life predictions (80% accuracy)
  • IoT-based storage compatibility monitoring
  • Health-conscious recipe recommendations
  • Mobile-first React Native application
Methodology

Our Research Approach

Four integrated modules powered by AI, IoT, and machine learning

1

Real-Time Spoilage Detection

Image-Based: MobileNetV2 model deployed via React Native app captures and classifies food as Fresh or Spoiled with 90% confidence threshold. Uses Flask backend with TensorFlow Lite for mobile optimization.

Gas Sensor-Based: MQ-135 detects ammonia emissions (NH‚ÇÉ), DHT22 monitors temperature and humidity. ESP32 WiFi module transmits data every 30 minutes via MQTT protocol. Multi-parameter decision rules evaluate spoilage risk.

2

Shelf Life Estimation

Fruits/Vegetables: EfficientNetB2 with custom head and Test-Time Augmentation (TTA) achieves 80% accuracy across 30 freshness stages for 6 foods (apples, bananas, bell peppers, bitter gourds, carrots, tomatoes). Q10 formula adjusts for real-time sensor data.

Homemade Foods: Rule-based system using USDA/WHO/FDA guidelines (3-4 days fridge, 2 hours room temp for meat curry) + survey data from 100+ people aged 30+.

Packaged Foods: Tesseract OCR with preprocessing (contrast enhancement, adaptive thresholding) extracts expiry dates with 90% accuracy on 150 samples.

3

Storage Compatibility Management

IoT sensors (DHT22 for temp/humidity, MQ-135 for ethylene) monitor storage environment. Support Vector Machine (SVM) model with MinMaxScaler predicts spoilage risk (Will Spoil/Will Not Spoil). Rule-based compatibility matrix identifies incompatible food pairings (e.g., ethylene producers vs. sensitive items). RNN-based temporal analysis forecasts spoilage trends using historical data. Critical alerts sent when ethylene exceeds 0.5 ppm near sensitive items.

4

Recipe Suggestion System

TensorFlow Keras food risk model considers user health factors: allergies, BMI, cholesterol, blood pressure, lifestyle habits (alcohol, smoking). TF-IDF vectorization with cosine similarity matches expiring ingredients to 1000+ recipes database. Fuzzy matching (RapidFuzz) filters unsafe ingredients. Health-conscious filtering ensures safe recommendations for chronic conditions. Top 5 recipes provided with ingredients, instructions, and health warnings. Reduces waste by prioritizing expiring items.
